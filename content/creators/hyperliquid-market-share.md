Hyperliquid's share of decentralized perpetual volume rose from 18% to 31% between January and June 2026. The shift is not driven by a single catalyst: lower effective spreads, consistent uptime during volatility spikes, and a builder program that routes order flow to integrated frontends compound the effect.

Three onchain signals explain retention. First, median taker fees on comparable size buckets undercut major CEX perp books after accounting for funding drag. Second, top market-maker wallets show lower inventory turnover than on competing venues, suggesting deeper resting liquidity. Third, net USDC inflows to the clearing layer correlate with new wallet cohorts that stay active past 30 days.

Risk factors remain: single-chain dependency, limited spot depth for hedging basis, and governance concentration among early validators. This report maps wallet clusters, funding arbitrage desks, and the top 15 fee-generating addresses over the last 90 days.
